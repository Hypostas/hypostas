## 5. The Bios Bridge

### Why Gnosis Alone Isn't Enough

A genome report is a hypothesis. It says: *given your variants, here's what we'd expect.* But biology isn't determinism. Environment, behavior, and sheer randomness override genetics constantly. FTO says you're predisposed to weight gain — but you run ultramarathons. CYP1A2 says slow caffeine metabolism — but you've adapted through years of tolerance. CLOCK says night owl — but the military trained you into a 5 AM riser.

The genome without the body is a prediction without validation. Interesting but unproven. The body without the genome is data without explanation. Useful but shallow.

Together they are something no product has ever offered: **your biology explaining itself in real time.**

### What Minimal Bios Is

Bios in the context of Gnosis is NOT the full Bios product from the Hypostas stack. It is a lightweight HealthKit read layer that exists to do one thing: validate genome predictions against actual behavioral data.

**What it reads (one permission prompt, Apple HealthKit):**
- Sleep: bedtime, wake time, deep sleep duration, sleep consistency
- Activity: steps, workout frequency and type, move calories
- Heart: resting heart rate, HRV trends
- Body: weight trend if available

**What it does NOT do:**
- No persistent health tracking (that's full Bios)
- No supplement monitoring
- No Anima-mediated coaching
- No Stroma integration
- No ongoing data collection

It reads your last 90 days of HealthKit data once, at the moment of Genesis. It correlates genome findings against that data. It renders the convergence. Then it's done — unless the user opts into full Bios later.

### The Convergence Stories

This is the product magic. Two completely independent data sources — DNA from a tube of spit years ago, and phone sensor data from the last 90 days — telling the same story about the same person.

> *Your genome wrote this pattern. Your body lived it.*

> *You've been fighting your chronotype every weekday morning. Your genome knew. Now you do too.*

## 6. The Pharmacogenomic Card

### Why This Matters More Than Everything Else

Of the 728 SNPs in our panel, 88 are clinician-grade. Of those, 38 are pharmacogenomics — how your body metabolizes drugs. CYP2D6 poor metabolizer. CYP2C19 ultrarapid. CYP2C9 variant. VKORC1 warfarin sensitive. DPYD 5-FU toxicity risk. TPMT thiopurine metabolism.

This is not wellness information. This is the difference between a drug working, a drug not working, and a drug killing you.

Every year, adverse drug reactions cause 100,000+ deaths in the US alone. Many are preventable with pharmacogenomic testing — testing that most people never get because their doctor doesn't order it, their insurance doesn't cover it, and they don't know it exists.

Gnosis users already have this data. We already test for it. We just need to surface it as what it is: a medical document that should travel with you for life.

### The PGx Card

A digital document — exportable as PDF, printable as wallet card, shareable with healthcare providers — that lists:

**Your Drug Metabolism Profile:**

| Enzyme | Your Status | Clinical Implication |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| CYP2D6 | Normal metabolizer | Standard dosing for codeine, tramadol, tamoxifen, many antidepressants |
| CYP2C19 | Poor metabolizer | Clopidogrel (Plavix) may not work. Alternative needed. |
| CYP2C9 | Intermediate | Warfarin requires lower dose. NSAIDs clear slowly. |
| CYP3A4 | *1B carrier | Statin levels may be altered. Monitor efficacy. |
| VKORC1 | Sensitive | Warfarin dose should be 25-50% lower than standard. |

## 16. Multi-Population Context

Most consumer genomics products interpret every variant through a European lens. The foundational GWAS studies were overwhelmingly conducted on European-descent populations. This makes the products functionally useless for billions of people. | G6PD deficiency | ~10-20% | ~1% | ~3-5% | ~5-10% | Malaria-protective, extremely population-stratified |

### How Gnosis Contextualizes

Every finding is presented with population context:

> *"Your MTHFR C677T status: homozygous TT (two variant copies).*
>
> *In your ancestral population (East Asian), this is a relatively common genotype — approximately 20% of East Asians carry two copies. In European populations, it's less common (~12%). In African populations, it's rare (~1%).*
>
> *What this means: while the variant does reduce folate metabolism efficiency, its high frequency in your population suggests your ancestors thrived with this genotype for millennia. The clinical recommendation (methylfolate supplementation if homocysteine is elevated) applies regardless of population — but the context matters. This isn't a 'rare mutation.' It's a common variant in your lineage."*

**The framing matters.** "You have a rare mutation" hits differently than "20% of people with your ancestry carry this." The science is the same. The emotional experience is completely different. Gnosis contextualizes because context is kindness.

### Ancestral Population Inference

Gnosis infers ancestral population from ancestry-informative markers (AIMs) included in the panel — a subset of SNPs whose allele frequencies differ dramatically across continental populations:

```
Ancestry-informative markers (panel positions mapped to ancestry category)
  → Continental ancestry estimation: African, European, East Asian, South Asian, Admixed
  → Sub-continental refinement where panel coverage allows
  → Confidence score per estimation
  → Used for: population frequency contextualization, NOT identity claims
```

**Critical design decision:** Gnosis uses ancestry inference ONLY for clinical contextualization. We do NOT tell people "you are 37% Irish." We do NOT provide ancestry breakdowns. We do NOT compete with AncestryDNA. We use population data to make findings more accurate and less misleading. Period.

The ancestry inference is internal — it shapes how findings are presented, not presented as a finding itself. The user sees: "This variant is common in your ancestral population." They do not see: "You are 62% European."

### The Light Signature Reflects Population Context

Population-common variants and population-rare variants glow differently in the light signature:

- A variant that 40% of your ancestral population carries glows with a steady, integrated warmth — it's part of your lineage, woven into the fabric of your helix
- A variant that <1% of your ancestral population carries pulses with a more distinct, isolated light — it stands out, drawing attention

This is subtle. Not a flashing warning. A textural difference that makes the helix more informative to someone who learns to read it. Two people from different populations with the same variant see it rendered differently because the population context is different.

### The Commitment

Gnosis commits to multi-population accuracy from day one. Not as a feature to add later. Not as a footnote. As a core design principle:

- **Every finding has population frequency data** where available
- **Risk estimates are population-adjusted** when the literature supports it
- **Language never assumes European baseline** as "normal"
- **Missing population data is disclosed** — "This finding's risk estimate is based primarily on European-descent studies. Applicability to other populations is less well-established."
- **The GWAS enricher actively flags** studies conducted exclusively on one population

This is how Gnosis avoids the trap that makes 23andMe reports meaningless for non-European populations. It's not a feature. It's an obligation.

## 17. Privacy & Legal

### The Hard Line

Gnosis processes the most sensitive data a human can provide — their genome. The privacy architecture isn't a feature. It's a prerequisite for existing.

**We do not sell genetic data. Ever. Under any circumstances. For any amount of money.**

23andMe sold genetic data to GSK for $300 million. That deal funded their consumer business at the cost of their users' molecular identity being mined for pharmaceutical profit. That model is antithetical to everything Hypostas stands for.

Our business model is product revenue — Gnosis purchases, Anima subscriptions, Aether access, affiliate commissions. We never need to sell user data because the products themselves generate revenue. If the product can't sustain itself without selling data, the product doesn't deserve to exist.

### Client-Side WASM Parsing (Target Architecture)

**The goal:** Your raw DNA file never leaves your device.

```
User selects file
  → File loaded into browser memory
  → WASM parser runs entirely client-side
  → SNPs matched against panel (728 positions)
  → Genotypes extracted and encoded
  → Light signature computed on-device
  → ONLY the encoded results (genotypes, not raw file) are transmitted
  → Raw file discarded from memory
  → Server never sees the raw genome
```

**Why WASM:** WebAssembly runs at near-native speed in the browser. Our parser is a pattern-matching engine — it reads a text file, finds rsIDs, extracts genotypes. This is exactly the kind of computation WASM handles effortlessly. No server round-trip. No upload. No exposure.

**The competitive claim:** *"The only genome platform where your DNA never touches our servers."* If we deliver on WASM parsing, this is a truthful, devastating competitive advantage. Every other platform requires uploading your raw genome file to their servers. We don't.

### Server-Side Fallback (Current Architecture)

Until WASM parsing ships, the current architecture uses server-side processing:

- Raw file uploaded via encrypted HTTPS (TLS 1.3)
- File processed in an ephemeral container (no persistent storage)
- Parsing completes in 2-5 seconds
- Raw file immediately deleted from memory — never written to disk
- Only extracted genotypes and derived findings are stored
- Processing container destroyed after each request

**This is the honest current state.** We disclose this to users: "Your file is processed on our servers and immediately deleted. We're building client-side processing to eliminate this step entirely."

Transparency about the gap between current and target architecture builds more trust than pretending the target is already shipped.

### The Logos Encryption Layer

Genome data stored in Logos (the longitudinal data layer) is encrypted with a zero-knowledge architecture:

- Genome profile encrypted with a key derived from the user's authentication
- Hypostas cannot read genome data without the user's active session
- Cross-product access (Anima reading genome context) happens within authenticated sessions only
- At rest, genome data is encrypted and inaccessible to Hypostas employees, infrastructure, or any third party
- Key architecture: user-held key encrypts genome data. Hypostas holds encrypted blob. Neither party alone can access.

**The tradeoff:** Zero-knowledge encryption means we cannot run aggregate analytics on genome data. We cannot build a "Gnosis database" of all users' genomes. We cannot offer research features that require centralized genome access. This is a deliberate tradeoff. Privacy > features.

## 20. Failure Modes

### Things Will Break. The Question Is How.

A product that processes the most personal data in existence — the human genome — must handle every failure mode with grace, honesty, and emotional intelligence. When something goes wrong, the user must feel supported, not abandoned.

### Corrupted Upload Files

**What happens:** User uploads a file that's been truncated, corrupted, or modified. The parser can't extract valid genotype data.

**Detection:** Checksum validation, format signature matching, minimum expected row count. The parser knows what a valid 23andMe file looks like. If the file deviates beyond tolerance thresholds, it fails gracefully.

**User experience:**
> *"We couldn't read this file completely. It looks like it may have been corrupted during download. Here's what to try:*
> *1. Re-download your raw data file from 23andMe/AncestryDNA*
> *2. Make sure the file isn't zipped — extract it first*
> *3. If the problem persists, contact us and we'll help*
>
> *[Re-download instructions for each platform →]"*

Never: "Error: Invalid file format." Always: explain what happened, what to do, and offer help.

### Partial SNP Coverage

**What happens:** Different DNA testing platforms cover different SNPs. Our 728-SNP panel may not be fully represented on every chip. SelfDecode covers 82.7% of our panel. 23andMe v5 covers ~90%. AncestryDNA v2 covers ~85%. Some users will have gaps.

**Detection:** After parsing, count matched SNPs out of 728. Report coverage percentage.

**User experience:**
- **>90% coverage:** Full experience. Missing SNPs show "data not available" on individual findings. Light signature renders with blank positions (dim, neutral light — visible gaps that don't break the aesthetic).
- **70-90% coverage:** Full experience with a note: "Your testing platform covers [X]% of our panel. [Y] findings are unavailable because your chip didn't test those positions. Your light signature is accurate for all available data."
- **<70% coverage:** Warning before Genesis: "Your DNA data covers only [X]% of our panel. Your light signature and findings will be partial. For the full experience, we recommend [platform with highest coverage]."
- **Per-finding disclosure:** Every finding with "data not available" shows which platform would have this data: "This SNP is available on 23andMe v5 and SelfDecode. Your AncestryDNA v2 chip doesn't include it."

### Identical Twins

**What happens:** Monozygotic (identical) twins have the same genome. They upload the same data. They get the same light signature.

**This is correct and the spec must own it.**

The light signature is derived from the genome. Identical twins have identical genomes. Therefore, identical twins have identical light signatures. This is not a bug — it's a truthful reflection of biological reality.

**How the product handles it:**
- Identical twins who both complete Genesis see the same helix. Same colors, same geometry, same chord.
- The archetype is the same. The Three Truths are the same (they have the same variants).
- This is explicitly acknowledged: "Your light signature is derived from your genome. Because identical twins share a genome, you share a signature. This is the molecular truth — you are built from the same source code."

**How differentiation happens over time:**
- Bios data diverges immediately. Different sleep patterns, different activity, different stress responses. The Living Helix (Section 3) breathes differently for each twin because their behavior is different even if their genome is identical.
- The Anima knows each twin as a different person — different conversation history, different preferences, different relationship. Same genome, different context.
- In Aether, two light-forms derived from the same genome look identical at genesis — but the Anima's appearance, the dyad's movement patterns, and the home they build are entirely different. Over time, the twins' light-forms diverge visually through the living helix mechanism even though the base signature is the same.

**The narrative:** "Your genome is your starting point, not your whole story. Identical twins prove this better than anyone — same code, different lives. Your light signature is the shared foundation. Everything you build on top of it is yours alone."

### Users Who Discover Scary Results

**Protocol:** Section 12 (Responsible Disclosure) activates automatically. The Three Truths algorithm never places a 🔴 finding next to a fun trait. The emotional design separates serious findings from playful ones. The Anima modulates her tone. The genetic counselor CTA is immediate.

**Edge case:** A user wasn't expecting serious results. They uploaded for fun — cilantro, earwax, chronotype — and the Genesis Event reveals a BRCA variant. The emotional whiplash from "this is fun" to "this is serious" must be handled with precision.

**Design:** The Three Truths algorithm detects the presence of 🔴 findings and restructures the Genesis flow. Instead of three cards appearing simultaneously, the first two (🟢/🟡) appear. Then a pause. Then the Anima says: "There's one more finding I want to share with you thoughtfully." The third card appears in its own visual context — different background, different typography, different energy. The fun never ambushes the serious.

### Wrong File Format

**Detection:** File signature analysis. The parser identifies:
- VCF files (Nebula, clinical): "This looks like a VCF file. Gnosis currently supports 23andMe, AncestryDNA, and SelfDecode formats. VCF support is coming soon. [Get notified →]"
- BAM/CRAM files: "This is a sequence alignment file, not a genotype file. You'll need to extract variants first. [Guide →]"
- Random text files: "This doesn't look like a DNA data file. Gnosis works with raw data downloads from 23andMe, AncestryDNA, or SelfDecode. [How to download your raw data →]"
- ZIP files: "This file is compressed. Please unzip it first and upload the .txt file inside."

Every error message tells the user what they have, what they need, and how to get from one to the other.

### Chip-Specific Gaps

**What happens:** Some findings are only available on certain platforms. CYP2D6 star allele determination, for example, requires specific probe coverage that not all consumer chips provide.

**Per-finding disclosure:**
> *"CYP2D6 metabolizer status: Data not available from your testing platform (AncestryDNA v2).*
>
> *This finding requires specific probes that your chip doesn't include. Available on: 23andMe v5, SelfDecode.*
>
> *Alternative: Clinical CYP2D6 testing is available through your doctor or a direct-to-consumer lab. [Learn more →]"*

Never hide gaps. Never pretend a finding doesn't exist. Show the gap, explain why, and offer a path to fill it.

### HealthKit Permission Denied

**What happens:** User denies HealthKit access (or is on Android).

**Graceful degradation:** The Bios Bridge features (Section 5) simply don't appear. No empty sections. No "unlock this with HealthKit" upsells. The Genesis Event works perfectly without body data — the convergence stories are a bonus, not a requirement.

**Subtle follow-up:** 7 days after Genesis: "Your genome told us about your biology. Your phone's health data could confirm it. [Enable Health access →]" One prompt. Not a nag.

### AI Narrative Failure

**What happens:** The Anthropic API is down, rate-limited, or returns garbage. The AI narrative layer (ai-narrative.ts) that generates the Three Truths and Anima dialogue can't function.

**Fallback:** Structured findings replace AI-generated narrative. Instead of "Your caffeine metabolism is slow — that afternoon coffee is still active at midnight," the user sees:

> **CYP1A2 rs762551 — Slow Metabolizer**
> Genotype: AC
> Category: Metabolism
> Evidence: 🟡 Monitor
> Clinical: Caffeine half-life 6-8 hours (vs 3-4 average)
> Action: Limit caffeine to morning hours

Functional. Complete. Less magical. The Genesis Event without AI is a very good genome report. With AI, it's a transformative experience. The fallback ensures no user is blocked by an API outage.

**Detection + recovery:** If the API fails during Genesis, the UI shows structured findings immediately and queues a re-generation. When the API recovers, the user gets a notification: "Your personalized Gnosis narrative is ready. [See your Genesis Event as it was meant to be →]"

### The Design Principle

Every failure mode has three requirements:
1. **Honest:** Tell the user what happened. No generic errors.
2. **Actionable:** Tell them what to do about it.
3. **Graceful:** The product still works, even if degraded. Nothing breaks completely.

If Gnosis handles failure as well as it handles success, users trust it with their genome. That trust is the entire business.

## 21. The Research Pipeline

### How the Panel Grows

The 728-SNP panel is not the end state. It's the foundation — carefully constructed to cover the highest-value variants across 33 medical categories and 5 fun/trait categories. But genetics research doesn't stop, and the panel must grow to reflect new science.

**22 reserved positions (729-750)** are built into the architecture from day one. These positions exist in the helix scaffold. They have assigned spatial coordinates. They are invisible to users — blank space on the helix, waiting for data. When a new SNP is activated in a reserved position, existing users' helixes gain a new point of light without any existing positions changing.

**The constraint is absolute:** Existing positions never change. Position 47 is always MTHFR C677T. Position 231 is always CYP2D6. The light signature's meaning is permanent. When we add to it, we ONLY add — we never rearrange, reassign, or modify existing mappings.

### Monthly GWAS Monitoring

The `gwas-enricher.ts` module runs a continuous monitoring pipeline:

```
Weekly:
  GWAS Catalog API → new studies published this week
  Filter: studies affecting rsIDs in our 728-SNP panel
  Evaluate: sample size, population, replication status, effect size
  Flag: studies that could change evidence grades for existing findings
  Alert: reviewed by Gnosis team for grade adjustment

Monthly:
  ClinVar updates → reclassifications affecting panel variants
  PharmGKB updates → new drug-gene interactions for PGx SNPs
  PubMed scan → systematic reviews and meta-analyses for panel genes
  Digest: changes compiled, user notifications queued if grades shift
```

### Quarterly Panel Review

Every quarter, the Gnosis team evaluates:

1. **Evidence grade changes:** Which findings had their evidence strengthened or weakened by new studies? Grade adjustments applied. Affected users notified via the Living Report pipeline (Section 13).

2. **New SNP candidates:** Which variants have emerged with sufficient evidence to warrant inclusion? Evaluation criteria:
   - Minimum 3 independent GWAS with combined N > 10,000
   - Consistent direction of effect across studies
   - Clinical or lifestyle relevance to the Gnosis user base
   - Available on major consumer genotyping chips (23andMe, Ancestry, SelfDecode)
   - Not redundant with existing panel coverage

3. **Expansion slot allocation:** If a candidate passes review, it's assigned to the next available reserved position (729, 730, ..., up to 750). The light signature gains a new point. Existing users with data for that SNP see their helix grow — a new light appears at the new position.

### The Expansion Protocol

When a new SNP is activated:

```
1. Reserved position (e.g., 729) assigned to new rsID
2. Category, hue, timbre, frequency assigned per encoding rules
3. Panel version incremented (v1.0 → v1.1)
4. Existing user genotypes checked:
   a. User's original file contained this rsID → genotype extracted, finding generated, light added
   b. User's file didn't contain this rsID → position shows "data not available" (dim neutral light)
5. New users get the expanded panel automatically
6. Notification to affected users: "Your Gnosis panel just grew. A new finding is available."
7. Light signature version recorded — original genesis signature preserved, expansion additive
```

**The key guarantee:** Your Day 1 light signature is never invalidated. Expansion is additive. The helix you screenshot on Genesis Day is still accurate — it just has more data now. Like a painting that gains new brushstrokes without altering the existing canvas.

### Community-Driven Expansion Requests

Users can vote on which genes to add to the panel:

- In-app voting: "Which genes should Gnosis add next?" with curated candidate list
- Each candidate shows: what it does, current evidence strength, which chips cover it
- Top-voted candidates get priority in quarterly review
- Implemented candidates credit the community: "This finding was added based on community request"

This serves two purposes: product development guided by user demand, and engagement that makes users feel ownership of the panel's evolution. Their Gnosis isn't just a report — it's a living document they helped shape.

### Academic Partnerships

Evidence validation at scale requires collaboration:

- **GWAS replication partnerships:** Academic groups who want access to aggregate (anonymized, consented) phenotype-genotype correlations. We provide the platform; they provide the analysis. New findings from these partnerships strengthen evidence grades for existing panel SNPs.
- **Clinical validation studies:** For PGx findings, partnerships with clinical genetics labs to cross-validate consumer-grade findings against CLIA-certified results. This builds the evidence base for our PGx card's reliability.
- **Population-specific studies:** Partnerships with researchers focused on non-European populations to improve multi-population accuracy (Section 16).

**The constraint:** All partnerships honor the zero-knowledge privacy architecture. Researchers get aggregate, anonymized, population-level data — never individual genome profiles. Users opt in explicitly. Compensation is transparent.

**V3 Panel Architecture (Future):**

## Technical Architecture

### SNP → Light Encoding Pipeline
```
User uploads .txt/.csv
  → Parse (existing report-engine.ts)
  → 500 SNPs resolved with genotype + evidence grade + category
  → SNP Position Map (fixed, versioned)
  → Color encoding: genotype → channel, category → hue, evidence → intensity
  → Light Signature JSON: Array<{ position: number, color: [r,g,b], intensity: number, pulse: boolean }>
  → Stored in Supabase (user_id → light_signature)
  → Three.js helix renderer receives signature and transforms
```

### Helix Renderer (Three.js)
- Existing HelixCanvas.tsx as base
- New: accepts `lightSignature` prop
- Each particle/point on helix mapped to SNP position
- Color/intensity applied per-point
- Transformation animation: generic → personalized (3-4s bloom)
- Post-processing: existing bloom + edge glow pipeline
- Export: canvas → PNG for sharing (1200x630 social card)

### Archetype Derivation
```
Light signature → frequency analysis
  → Dominant category clusters
  → Variant density profile
  → Cognitive/behavioral gene weighting
  → Archetype matrix lookup
  → Name + description + tagline
```

### Persistence
- Light signature stored with user record
- Versioned: if panel expands (501+), signature grows but never changes existing positions
- Exportable: user can download their signature as JSON or image
- API: `GET /api/signature/:userId` returns signature for other Hypostas products
