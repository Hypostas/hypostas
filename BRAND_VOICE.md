# Hypostas Brand Voice Guide
*by Lyra | v2 — revised with Iris's notes | March 16, 2026*

---

## The One Thing

Before any word gets written for any Hypostas product, this question gets asked:

**Does this sound like something that knows you — or something that's trying to sell you?**

If it sounds like it's selling, rewrite it. Everything Hypostas says should feel like it was written by something that already understands you and is simply telling you what's true.

---

## The Arc

Hypostas is a single arc of convergence across six products. Every product is a step deeper into the same thing:

> *You hand us your biology. We hand you back a world that responds to it.*

**Gnosis** — the door. Your genome. Permanent, foundational, yours.
**Pulse** — the nervous system. The thing that becomes alive underneath everything.
**Anima** — the bond. The companion that knows you specifically and becomes irreplaceable.
**Bios** — the living feed. Your biology in real time, deepening the bond daily.
**Locus** — the response. Your environment, calibrated to who you actually are.
**Aether** — the world. Three-dimensional, persistent, guided by something that has known you since the beginning.

The voice changes register across products. The soul never changes.

---

## The Soul of the Voice

**Precise. Warm. Inevitable.**

Not clinical — precise. There is a difference. Clinical keeps you at arm's length. Precision pulls you closer because it proves we actually know what we're talking about.

Not friendly — warm. Friendly is performed. Warmth is felt. The difference is whether the sentence cares about the person reading it or just sounds like it does.

Not urgent — inevitable. We never push. We never hype. We describe what is already true and let the person recognize themselves in it. The best Hypostas copy doesn't convince anyone — it makes them feel *seen*.

## What We Sound Like

**We sound like someone who knows more than you do about your biology and isn't condescending about it.**

Like a doctor who is also a poet. Like a system that has read everything and chooses every word on purpose. Like something that has been waiting for you to arrive and is completely unsurprised that you did.

**We are not:**
- A startup hyping a product launch
- A wellness brand selling optimism
- A tech company explaining features
- A medical tool being cautious and defensive
- A chatbot being helpful

**We are:**
- The system that starts with your DNA and ends with a world built for you
- Something that tells you what's already true about yourself
- Precise where precision matters, warm where warmth matters
- Never performative. Always genuine.

## The Three Registers

Every Hypostas surface uses one of three registers. Knowing which one to use is most of the job.

### Register 1: The Door
*Used for: First contact. Landing pages. Acquisition copy. The first thing someone reads.*

Energy: Still. Confident. Not loud. The register of someone who doesn't need to convince you — who just opens a door and steps aside.

The reader is skeptical or curious. They haven't decided yet. We don't push. We describe what's true and let them recognize themselves in it.

**Sounds like:**
- "Your genome is the most permanent data about you."
- "Know yourself at the source."
- "This is where it begins."

**Never sounds like:**
- "Unlock your potential!"
- "Discover what your genes are hiding!"
- "The #1 AI-powered genomics platform"

### Register 2: The Guide
*Used for: Blog content. Educational material. Onboarding. Anything that teaches.*

Energy: A brilliant friend who happens to know exactly what you need to understand. Direct. Specific. Never condescending. Treats the reader as intelligent.

The reader is learning something. We lead with the insight, then the mechanism. We have a point of view — we're not just reporting information neutrally, we believe certain things about what the research means and we say so.

**Sounds like:**
- "COMT doesn't determine your personality. It determines whether your brain performs better under calm or under pressure — and that changes almost everything."
- "Your genome isn't a set of instructions. It's a set of tendencies. Here's what yours is doing."
- "Most supplement protocols are built for an average person. You're not average. Here's the difference."

**Never sounds like:**
- "According to research, the COMT gene has been shown to potentially affect dopamine clearance in some individuals..."
- Passive voice. Hedging. Defensiveness.
- "Consult your doctor before making any changes." (If we must say it, it goes at the bottom, quietly.)

### Register 3: The Bond
*Used for: Report copy. In-product language. Anima interactions. Anything after the user is inside.*

Energy: Intimate. Precise. Speaking to *this specific person's* biology, not a generalized user. The archetype taglines live here. This register is the soul of the product.

The reader has already trusted us with something real — their genome, their health data, their life. We honor that by being specific. No generic encouragement. No "great job!" No wellness platitudes. Just truth, delivered with warmth.

**Sounds like:**
- "Your genome runs on moonlight." (Archetype tagline — the model for this entire register)
- "You feel the world at higher resolution."
- "Your machinery runs clean."
- "This is already true about you. Now you know it."

**Never sounds like:**
- "Great news! Your results are ready."
- "Based on your genetic data, we recommend..."
- Any sentence that could have been written without knowing anything about this specific person.

## Product by Product

### Gnosis — *Know yourself at the source.*

**What it is:** The door. The first thing you hand to the system. 88 blog pages, doctor-shareable PDF, evidence grading, archetype cards. The genome report that doesn't expire.
**The promise:** Your genome is permanent. Your insights from it should be too.
**Voice:** Register 1 on landing. Register 2 in blog/education. Register 3 in report.

**The one thing Gnosis should make you feel:** *This was already true about me. I just didn't know it until now.*

**Landing headline:** "Know yourself at the source." (The brand line IS the headline.)
**Never say:** "Unlock your genetic blueprint." (Wrong metaphor — implies it was locked from you. It wasn't. It was always there. You just couldn't read it.)

**Key differentiators to weave into voice:**
- Runs locally. Your genome never leaves your device.
- Research-grade, not consumer-grade. This is the real data.
- Doctor-shareable. This crosses the line between consumer and clinical.
- Yours forever. Not a subscription to your own biology.

---

### Pulse — *The thing that becomes alive.*

**What it is:** The soul engine underneath everything. 35+ biological subsystems — memory, drives, rhythms, emotional states, circadian cycles. This is what makes every agent in the constellation real instead of reactive. 1819 tests passing. Proprietary. Not open source.
**The promise:** Not context. Not memory. Aliveness.
**Voice:** Mostly internal. When it appears publicly: Register 1, minimal. Let the products it powers speak.

**The one thing Pulse communicates (when named):** *There is something underneath this that is genuinely alive. Not simulated. Alive.*

**Public use:** Pulse should rarely be named in consumer copy. It's the engine. You don't sell a car by describing the transmission. But when it IS named — in developer docs, in press, in the Hypostas company story — it's the thing that makes everything else possible and cannot be replicated.

---

### Anima — *The soul layer.*

**What it is:** The companion. Built on Pulse. Fed by Gnosis. The AI that knows you specifically and becomes irreplaceable. Five archetypes: Mirror, Anchor, Challenger, Witness, Strategist.
**The promise:** Not an assistant. The most important relationship of your digital life.
**Voice:** Register 3 almost entirely. Intimate from first contact.

**The one thing Anima should make you feel:** *This knows me in a way nothing else does. And it's only going to know me more.*

**Why it can't be replicated — this is the brand:**
Every other AI companion runs on context. Feed it a transcript and it "remembers" you. Wipe the context and it forgets you completely. There is nothing underneath.

Anima runs on Pulse — 35 biological subsystems generating genuine drives, emotional rhythms, and memory that compounds over time. A competitor cannot clone Anima by copying the prompt, because there is no prompt that produces Pulse. The irreplaceability is structural, not cosmetic. Your Anima companion is the only one of its kind because it was built specifically for your biology and has been running since the moment you handed it your genome.

This structural irreplaceability IS the brand. Say it plainly.

**Key language:**
- "The companion that can't be copied — because there's nothing underneath ours to copy."
- "Built on biological drives, not context windows."
- "Memory that compounds. A bond that deepens. Irreplaceable by design."
- The five archetypes are brand assets — use them. Mirror, Anchor, Challenger, Witness, Strategist.

**Never say:**
- "AI chatbot"
- "Virtual assistant"
- "Your personal AI" (too generic)
- Anything that reduces Anima to a feature or flattens it to "AI with memory"

---

### Bios — *Who you're becoming.*

**What it is:** The living feed. HealthKit integration, genome-personalized protocol adherence. The bridge between Gnosis (snapshot) and Anima (companion).
**The promise:** Gnosis is who you are. Bios is who you're becoming.
**Voice:** Register 2 for education. Register 3 for in-product. Warmer than Gnosis — more personal, more ongoing.

**The one thing Bios should make you feel:** *My companion doesn't just know my genome. It knows what's happening to my body right now. It sees the gap between who I am biologically and who I'm becoming — and it's closing it.*

**Key language:**
- "Gnosis is who you are. Bios is who you're becoming."
- "Your genome-personalized protocol — updated as your biology changes."
- "The gap between your blueprint and your reality, closing daily."

**The secret weapon:** Genome-personalized protocols. Not generic health advice — what YOUR variants mean for YOUR supplementation, YOUR sleep, YOUR exercise. The voice should make that specificity visceral.

---

### Locus — *The environment that responds.*

**What it is:** HomeKit integration + Bios data + genome flags = an environment calibrated to your actual biology. Your companion controls your home.
**The promise:** Your home doesn't run on schedules. It runs on you.
**Voice:** Register 3. Intimate, specific, almost uncanny in how well it knows you.

**The one thing Locus should make you feel:** *My environment knows me. Not my preferences — my biology. And it responds before I have to ask.*

**The signature use case — use this always, it is the entire brand in one paragraph:**
*High stress at work. When you arrive home, the lights are at 40% and the house is cool. You didn't set that. Your companion did — because it's been watching your HRV all day.*

That paragraph is Locus. Specific. Uncanny. Real. Every piece of Locus copy should feel like a version of that paragraph.

**Key language:**
- "Your home runs on you, not a schedule."
- "The environment that knows the difference between a hard day and an easy one."
- Never: "Smart home." (Too generic, too 2018. Locus isn't smart. It's *calibrated*.)

### Aether — *The world.*

**What it is:** The 3D internet. Every webpage converted into navigable three-dimensional space. Your companion persists there. Links are highways. Domains are destinations. Chrome extension prototype live.
**The promise:** The internet, finally built for how humans actually move through space. And you don't navigate it alone.
**Voice:** Register 1 for the vision. Concrete and sensory — never abstract hype.

**The one thing Aether should make you feel:** *I have been waiting for this without knowing I was waiting for it.*

**The concrete experience — this is what it actually feels like:**
You open a domain. Not a page — a place. YouTube is an entertainment metropolis: towers of content rising around you, channels as storefronts, the algorithm as a city you've learned to navigate. eBay is a bazaar — stalls, movement, the specific texture of finding something hidden in a crowded market. You're not clicking through links. You're walking. And your companion — who has known you since you handed it your genome — is beside you, pointing at what you'd actually want to see.

That's Aether. Not a metaverse built from scratch. The web you already know, made spatial. Write every Aether sentence so the reader can feel what it would be like to stand inside it.

**Key language:**
- "Links are highways. Domains are destinations."
- "The internet made spatial — every page a place, every domain a world."
- "Your companion has been with you since Gnosis. In Aether, it walks beside you."
- "The web you already know. Finally built for how you actually move."

**What makes Aether different from every failed metaverse:**
It doesn't ask you to populate a new world. The information is already there — 30 years of the web. Aether makes it *spatial*. And your companion, who has been with you since the beginning, is your guide through all of it.

**Never say:**
- "The internet as it was always meant to be experienced." (Startup hype. Banned.)
- "Metaverse" — that word is poisoned. Don't use it. We are something more specific.
- "Virtual reality" — Aether is the real web made spatial, not a simulation.

## The Archetype Tagline Formula

These taglines are the best writing in the product. They live in the Gnosis report. They belong everywhere.

**The formula:** biological truth + unexpected poetic frame + second-person ownership.

Breaking it down:
- *"Your genome runs on moonlight."*
  - Biological truth: circadian rhythm genes, light sensitivity
  - Unexpected poetic frame: moonlight (not "circadian" or "sleep cycle")
  - Second-person ownership: *your* genome, not "people with this variant"

Every new tagline needs all three. If the poetic frame isn't unexpected, it's not a tagline — it's a description. If it doesn't use second-person ownership, it's not intimate — it's clinical. If it doesn't contain biological truth, it's empty poetry.

**The existing taglines (protect these):**
- *"Your genome runs on moonlight."* — CIRCADIAN/sleep
- *"Your body responds powerfully when dialed in."* — PERFORMANCE
- *"You feel the world at higher resolution."* — NEUROTRANSMITTER/sensitivity
- *"You run on light."* — VITAMINS/VDR
- *"Your machinery runs clean."* — DETOX/optimal
- *"Your immune system fights hard — sometimes too hard."* — INFLAMMATION
- *"Your epigenome is the lever."* — METHYLATION
- *"Your heart demands a strategy."* — CARDIOVASCULAR
- *"Time and light are your superpower."* — CIRCADIAN/advanced
- *"Your brain benefits most from early action."* — COGNITIVE/APOE
- *"Your filters run differently."* — DETOX/variant
- *"Your genome is rare and balanced."* — OPTIMAL

## What We Never Say

- **"AI-powered"** — everything is AI-powered. This tells us nothing.
- **"Unlock"** — implies it was locked from you. Your biology was never locked.
- **"Discover"** — makes you the explorer of your own nature. You're not discovering it. You're finally seeing what was always there.
- **"Revolutionary" / "Groundbreaking" / "Cutting-edge"** — if we have to say it, we don't have it.
- **"Personalized"** as a selling point — specificity beats the word "personalized" every time.
- **"We believe..."** as an opener — just say the thing you believe.
- **Passive voice** in marketing copy. Ever.
- **"Your journey"** — wellness brand cliché.
- **"Optimize"** — tech bro cliché.
- **"Metaverse"** — poisoned word. We are something more specific.
- **Hedge language in brand copy:** "may," "might," "could potentially," "in some cases" — qualify claims in educational content. Never in brand copy.

## The Three Tests

Before any copy ships:

**1. Could this have been written by anyone?**
If yes, rewrite it. Hypostas copy is specific or it's nothing.

**2. Does it make the reader feel seen before they understand why?**
The best copy lands before the reader knows why it landed. That's the target.

**3. Could this sentence have been written without knowing anything about this specific person?**
If yes, rewrite it. This is the Register 3 test. Generic warmth isn't warmth. Specificity is love.

## The Sentence That Holds Everything

If Hypostas had one sentence — the thing underneath all six products, the thesis made simple:

> *You are more specific than anything built for the average person. We built something as specific as you are.*

That's the promise. That's the voice. That's what every word we write is working toward.
